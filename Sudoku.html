Pair 		hodoku hodoku createclassicsudoku If you can find two cells, both in the same house, that have only the same two candidates left, you can eliminate that two candidates from all other cells in that house. If the two cells that form the Naked Pair are not only confined to one but to two houses (a row and a block or a column and a block), they are sometimes called a Locked Pair. Candidates can be eliminated from both houses.
NT Naked Triple Locked Triple hodoku hodoku If you can find three cells, all in the same house, that have only the same three candidates left, you can eliminate that candidates from all other cells in that house. It is important to note that not all cells must contain all three candidates, but there must not be more than three candidates in the three cells all together. If the three cells that form the Naked Triple are not only confined to one but to two houses (a row and a block or a column and a block), they are sometimes called a Locked Triple.
